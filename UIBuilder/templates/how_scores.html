Plain language

How the risk score works

The app shows a 0–100 number called the True Threat Detection Score (TDS). It’s built from public data only — not a personal safety guarantee and not a replacement for NWS or your county.

{% include '_guest_ai_nudge.html' %}

In one sentence

We measure several hazards (water, wind, inland flood signals, nearby road trouble), turn each into a 0–10 strength, combine them with weights, then multiply by a factor from your official evacuation zone when the map matches your pin. The final number is capped at 100.

What the tiers mean

  • Low (green) — Nothing in the blended feeds is pushing the index up right now. Keep normal season habits and check official sources when weather is active.
  • Elevated / high / extreme — One or more inputs are elevated; use it as a cue to read alerts, water levels, and county guidance — not as an order to evacuate.

Rough weight mix (Tampa Bay defaults)

Weights are approximate; the live code may tune inputs.

  • Coastal water — ~40% (tide / anomaly style signals)
  • Wind — ~30% (gusts, model / observation blends)
  • Inland flood — ~15% (alerts, rivers, rain signals)
  • Road / route friction — ~10% (public incident layers near you)

Your evacuation zone letter (A–E) from county or state GIS nudges the score up or down via a multiplier — planning context only.

Limits

Gauges break, models disagree, and buffers don’t match every driveway. County definitions and mandatory orders always win over any number in the app.
