At a glance

The essentials—expand a section below for context and detail.

Rainfall & wind

Forecast rain totals and peak gusts help estimate runoff and marine conditions. Values blend short-range model output (Open-Meteo) with what official grids expect (NWS).

Coastal water & seas

Tide-gauge height and departure from a recent average hint at surge, setup, or barometric effects. Offshore buoys report combined sea state.

Rivers & inland water

Stream gage height and flow show whether local waterways are elevated—relevant for riverine flooding away from the coast.

Alerts & forecast emphasis

NWS issues watches, warnings, and advisories for your grid point. We also surface how much rain the hourly forecast emphasizes (probability of precipitation).

Terrain reference

Ground elevation at the hub’s reference point is a static geographic cue only—it does not change with the storm. Pair it with surge and rainfall when thinking about compound risk.

How the index is built

The readiness index is a weighted blend of signals from the feeds above. It is a planning aid, not an official evacuation or safety decision. Always follow NWS and your county emergency managers.
